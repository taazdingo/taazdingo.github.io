An outer space simulation where players learn about Physics and Astronomy while repairing satellites in handcrafted levels.

Story and Narrative

It's 2103 AD and there's no fossil fuels left on Earth. New satellites can't be launched, neither could humans be sent to repair the ones in orbit. Dynaspace Corp. offers to save whatever's left by developing the innovative O.S.M.O.S.

On-Orbit Satellite Maintenance and Operation Spacecraft is uncrewed spacecraft used to repair and maintain satellites in orbit

"Equipped with a music player to warm things up for a rescued Martian!"

-Dynaspace Corp.

It is the player's job to remotely operate OSMOS to carry out each task they are given. As the levels progress, some secrets are uncovered in the dark of space and a macabre story unfolds.

Game Mechanics

Realistic physics and intuitive controls let the player cruise across orbits or cautiously dock into satellites without discursion.

There are two modes the player can switch between. Flight mode and Repair mode.

Flight

OSMOS is equipped with boosters in X,Y and Z axes to allow the player to thrust forward or slow down (retrograde burn).

OSMOS can be rotated freely using the mouse or the analog stick.

To avoid motion sickness, players may stop OSMOS's movement by pressing the Stabilize button.

Repair

Repair mode uses the same controls as Flight mode for different functions.

Once in the Repair mode, player gets access to the two mechanical arms.

Miscellaneous

OSMOS is equipped with a high-power flashlight that can be toggled on/off.

Depending on the level progression, other features like backup power supply, speed boost and music player are unlocked.

Level Design

Level progression in OSMOS goes hand-in-hand with mechanical progression and the story. Main elements of progression are

  • OSMOS abilities
  • New Tasks
  • Story fragments
  • Complexity of Tasks

Each mission includes a combination of smaller tasks. Tasks include:

  • Navigation (A to B)
  • Electrical/Mechanical repair
  • Orbital Surveillance
  • Photographing space and the planet
  • Scavenging for parts
  • Occasional alien rescue
